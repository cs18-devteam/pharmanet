Hypertension: Prevention and Treatment Options

Dr. Emily Rodriguez  •  October 10, 2024  •  7 min read

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, affects millions of people worldwide. Understanding its causes and treatment options is essential for prevention and management.

What is Hypertension?

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against artery walls. Hypertension occurs when this pressure is consistently too high (140/90 mmHg or higher).

Risk Factors

Several factors increase the risk of developing hypertension:

  • Age (risk increases with age)
  • Family history
  • Obesity
  • High sodium diet
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Chronic stress

Lifestyle Changes

Before medication, lifestyle modifications can significantly reduce blood pressure:

  1. “Reduce Sodium”: Limit to less than 2,300mg per day.
  2. “Exercise Regularly”: Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly.
  3. “Maintain Healthy Weight”: Even small weight loss can help.
  4. “Limit Alcohol”: No more than 2 drinks per day.
  5. “Manage Stress”: Practice meditation, yoga, or deep breathing.
  6. “Eat Heart-Healthy Foods”: Focus on fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

Medications

If lifestyle changes aren’t enough, several medication classes can help:

  • ACE inhibitors
  • Beta-blockers
  • Calcium channel blockers
  • Diuretics
  • Combination medications

Regular monitoring and working with your healthcare provider is key to managing hypertension effectively.
